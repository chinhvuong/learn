# PRD 0001 — Language Learning App (MVP)

> Glossary: see `CONTEXT.md`. Decisions: see `docs/adr/0001`–`0005`.
> App name: **Inflow**. Target Language = English, Native Language = Vietnamese (parameterized).

## Problem Statement

People who want to learn English mostly quit because the material is boring and disconnected from their lives. Generic courses and flashcard apps ask them to grind vocabulary they don't care about, in a sequence someone else decided. Learners who *do* find content they love (an article, a YouTube video, a podcast) have no good way to turn it into structured learning — they look up words one by one, lose the thread, and stop. They also can't tell whether they're making progress, and there's nothing pulling them back tomorrow.

## Solution

An app built on **Comprehensible Input**: learners acquire English by consuming a large amount of interesting, understandable content, not by drilling. Its signature ability is turning content the learner already loves into a **Lesson** — extracting the **Vocabulary**, **Chunks**, and **Grammar Points** worth learning, a tap-to-reveal **Bilingual Passage**, and a source-specific **Practice Mode** (e.g. **Listening Replay** for audio). The moment a learner finishes a Lesson, the app recommends the next one — explaining *why* — to keep the momentum going.

New users start instantly on curated **Series** matched to their self-selected **Level**, beginning with a hand-crafted **Golden First Lesson** that guarantees an early win before they're ever asked to sign up. Progress is made visible and addictive through a cumulative **North Star** (Items Absorbed), a self-set **Daily Goal**, a **Streak**, and tiered **Celebration moments**. A separate **Challenge Feed** of swipeable, teleprompter-style videos drives engagement and viral acquisition.

Free learners get a full **Starter Series** plus a monthly allowance of **Creation Credits**; the **Paid Plan** ($16/mo or ~$119/yr) removes limits, unlocks podcast listening and offline download.

## User Stories

### Onboarding

1. As a new learner, I want a one-line promise on the welcome screen, so that I immediately understand what the app does for me.
2. As a new learner, I want to pick a few favorite topics, so that my recommendations feel personal from the very first Lesson.
3. As a new learner, I want to self-select my Reading Level using plain-language CEFR examples, so that I'm not forced to take a long placement test.
4. As a new learner, I don't want to be asked my Listening Level separately, so that onboarding stays short (the app seeds it as Reading − 1 band and self-corrects later).
5. As a new learner who is unsure of my level, I want a "let the app figure it out" option, so that I'm not blocked by a choice I can't make.
6. As a new learner, I want to complete a Golden First Lesson before signing up, so that I experience the value before committing.
7. As a new learner, I want to see my North Star jump and a session recap right after my first Lesson, so that I feel an immediate sense of progress.
8. As a new learner, I want to set a Daily Goal from a few presets (≈5/10/20 min), so that the Streak fits my life.
9. As a new learner, I want to sign up only after the aha moment, with Apple/Google/Email, so that registration feels like "saving my progress," not a gate.
10. As a new learner, I want my anonymous first-Lesson progress migrated into my account on signup, so that I don't lose what I just did.
11. As a new learner, I want to be asked for notification permission only after signup, with a priming screen explaining the benefit, so that I understand why before deciding.

### The core learning loop

12. As a learner, I want a Home screen whose biggest action is "Continue," so that I can re-enter my learning flow with one tap.
13. As a learner, I want "Continue" to resume the Lesson I left unfinished, and only recommend a new one if there's nothing in progress, so that I never lose my place.
14. As a learner, I want to see my cumulative North Star, today's Daily Goal progress, and my Streak on Home, so that I stay motivated.
15. As a learner, I want my two Levels (Reading and Listening) shown separately, so that my progress reflects that I read better than I listen.
16. As a learner, I want each Lesson to be short (~3–5 minutes), so that I get frequent completion and never feel overwhelmed.
17. As a learner reading a Lesson, I want the English text to be primary with translation revealed only on tap, so that I try to understand from context first.
18. As a learner reading a Lesson, I want tapping an unknown word or chunk to reveal its meaning and save it for learning, so that marking and understanding are one gesture.
19. As a learner reading a Lesson, I want words I leave untapped to count as "known," so that completing the Lesson requires no extra busywork.
20. As a learner, I want already-Absorbed Items highlighted in new text, so that I can see my accumulation in context.
21. As a learner reading a Lesson, I want to reveal the full sentence translation on demand, so that I can check myself when stuck.
22. As a learner with an audio Lesson, I want each sentence's audio to play with the transcript hidden by default, so that I practice real listening, not reading-along.
23. As a learner with an audio Lesson, I want to replay a sentence, slow it down, and replay an individual Item's audio span, so that I can train my ear precisely (Listening Replay).
24. As a beginner, I want an optional karaoke (read-along) mode for listening, so that I have a gentler on-ramp.
25. As a learner, I want a Lesson to count as completed only when I've processed every Item shown to me, so that my stats reflect real learning, not skimming.
26. As a learner, I want the next Lesson recommended immediately on completion, preloaded for one-tap continue, so that my momentum is preserved.
27. As a learner, I want each recommendation to show a human reason and a match %, so that I trust the app understands me.
28. As a learner who has met my Daily Goal, I want the option to rest guilt-free instead of being pushed to continue, so that the habit stays sustainable.
29. As a learner, I want a session recap (minutes studied, Items absorbed, skill-specific Level) on the completion screen, so that I see what I just accomplished.
30. As a learner, I want richer discovery (more recommendations, a Preference Tuner) below the fold on completion, so that the fast path stays default but I can deviate when I want.

### Recommendation & discovery

31. As a learner, I want the next Lesson to come from my current Series, then the curated pool, then my own imported Sources, so that there's always something relevant next.
32. As a learner, I want recommendations matched to my per-skill Level, so that content is never too hard or too easy.
33. As a learner, I want recommendations to reflect my topics, authors, keywords, and preferred formats, so that they match my taste.
34. As a learner, I want recommendations to occasionally serve a slightly easier Lesson to keep my momentum, so that I don't burn out on difficulty.
35. As a learner, I want to periodically tune my taste with quick yes/no on author, topic, and keyword, so that I steer future recommendations.
36. As a learner, I want a "Discover" surface to browse personalized Lessons myself, so that I can choose instead of being guided.
37. As a learner, I want Discover and the Challenge Feed kept clearly separate, so that learning and entertainment don't blur.

### Creating Lessons from my own content

38. As a learner, I want to paste a link (YouTube/podcast/article), paste text, or upload a file, so that I can learn from anything I find.
39. As a learner browsing the web or YouTube, I want to share content into the app to create a Lesson, so that capture is effortless (first-class share-sheet path).
40. As a learner, I want a long Source automatically split into several short Lessons, so that each is bite-sized.
41. As a learner, I want a previously-analyzed public Source to open instantly, so that I don't wait for work already done.
42. As a learner importing long audio, I want the analysis to run in the background and notify me when ready, so that I'm not stuck waiting.

45. As a learner pasting private text or files, I want it kept private to me, so that my personal content is never shared.
46. As a learner, I want different Lesson experiences by source type (Listening Replay for audio, Bilingual reading for text), so that the practice fits the medium.

### Gamification & progress

47. As a learner, I want my cumulative Absorbed Items as the headline number, so that I feel steady, lasting progress.
48. As a learner, I want a self-set Daily Goal to define my Streak, so that I can keep it honest on busy days.
49. As a learner, I want a Streak driven only by real learning (not by swiping Challenges), so that it means something.
50. As a learner, I want a small celebration folded into completion when I meet my Daily Goal, so that everyday wins feel good without fatigue.
51. As a learner, I want a full-screen Celebration with a shareable Milestone Card for big milestones (Streak 7/30/100, Level up, round North Star), so that rare moments feel special.
52. As a learner, I want to share Milestone Cards, so that I can show off progress.
53. As a learner, I want a profile that is primarily a trophy case, with "My Collection" of Absorbed Items as a secondary door, so that the app encourages me without turning into flashcard management.
54. As a learner, I want to see my Reading and Listening Levels progress toward the next CEFR band, so that I sense forward motion.
55. As a learner, I want a celebratory "Level up" moment when I cross a CEFR band, so that long-term progress is rewarded without a test.

### Review (light SRS)

56. As a learner, I want already-Absorbed Items to reappear naturally in new Lessons, so that I review through re-encounter rather than a chore.
57. As a learner, I want an optional 60-second quick review for rarely-seen Items, so that I can reinforce without pressure.
58. As a learner, I never want a red "due" badge piling up, so that review feels like an opportunity, not a debt.

### Challenge Feed

59. As a learner, I want a swipeable feed of short teleprompter-style Challenge videos at my Level, so that I can do light reading practice while relaxing.
60. As a learner, I want each Challenge to scroll text at a fixed pace with optional music/voice, so that I'm challenged to keep reading pace.
61. As a learner, I want a "go deeper on this topic" CTA at the end of a Challenge, so that I can move into real learning without it interrupting my swipe.
62. As a learner, I want to like, save, and share Challenges, so that I can engage and spread ones I like.

### Plans, paywall & offline

64. As a Free learner, I want a full Starter Series open to me, so that I can get genuinely hooked before paying.

68. As a learner, I want monthly ($16) and annual (~$119, ~35% off) options with annual highlighted, so that I can choose value.
69. As a Paid learner, I want unlimited consumption and greatly raised creation limits, so that I can learn freely.
70. As a Paid learner, I want to download Lessons (text and audio) for offline study, so that I can learn without signal.
71. As a learner, I want my progress recorded offline and synced when I reconnect, so that I lose nothing on the go.

### Settings & edge states

72. As a learner, I want to change my Daily Goal, Levels, and karaoke toggle, so that the app fits me over time.
73. As a learner, I want to manage my subscription, notifications, and offline downloads, so that I stay in control.
74. As a learner, I want clear, kind error states when creation fails, I lose signal, or content is rejected, so that I'm never confused or unfairly charged.

## Implementation Decisions

### Architecture & boundaries

- **Cross-platform mobile-first** (single codebase → iOS + Android); web app and web billing deferred. Backend is separate and language-agnostic so Target/Native Language are parameters, not hardcoded.
- **Three data layers** (ADR-0001):
  - *Shared derived layer*, cached once per unique Source: `SourceAnalysis` = objective `CandidateItem[]`, segmentation, derived metadata (topic/author/keyword/format), per-skill difficulty; plus Item/sentence **timestamps** (ADR-0002). Keyed by a **hybrid fingerprint** — normalized-URL fast path + content-hash authoritative.
  - *Importer-private layer*: full `BilingualPassage` text and full podcast transcript — never re-hosted to others.
  - *Per-learner layer*: `Lesson`, `LearnerItemStatus` (New/Learning/Known), `AbsorbedItem` (with SRS state), Interest Profile.
- **Personalization is a projection, not re-extraction**: every learner shares one objective `CandidateItem` set per Source; they differ only in `LearnerItemStatus` filtered by Level (the A2-vs-C1 split).

### Lesson Creation Engine (build first; also the content-production line)

- Use-case `createLesson(SourceInput) → Lesson[]` orchestrates ports: `ContentFetcher`, `Analyzer` (LLM), `GrammarMatcher`, `ChunkMatcher`, `Translator`, `AsrAligner`, `SourceCache`.
- Pipeline: **Ingest → Cache check → Moderation/Promotion gate → Analyze → Translate → Segment → Assemble → Index.**
- **Analyze is a staged pipeline** (ADR-0005): batch the cheap inventory-free steps (sentence split, Vocabulary extraction, metadata) in one call; separate the Inventory/expensive steps — grammar classification, chunk matching, translation — each cached/retried independently, with cheaper models on easy stages.
- **Grammar Points are a closed Grammar Inventory** the engine classifies against (ADR-0003); each entry carries CEFR + one authored Vietnamese explanation.
- **Chunks are hybrid** (ADR-0004): reference Chunk Inventory anchors (stable IDs/explanations/CEFR) + LLM-detected lower-confidence candidates; **all normalized to a canonical lemmatized form** for stable identity.
- **Segmentation**: a Source yields one Lesson by default; auto-split into multiple ~3–5-min Lessons only when it exceeds the target. Analyze the whole Source once (shared cache); segment as a cheap post-step.
- **Per-skill Source difficulty**: Reading difficulty (item-CEFR distribution + readability) and, for audio, a separate Listening difficulty (adds delivery features like speech rate). Both calibrated by learners' too-easy/too-hard flags.
- **ASR alignment** (ADR-0002): pre-generated for curated content during QA; lazy on first Listening Replay for user imports. Shared layer holds Item/sentence timestamps; full transcript stays importer-private.
- **Moderation/promotion** (ADR-0001): illegal/unsafe content blocked at the personal-use layer; a user Source enters the shared recommendation pool only after passing an automated moderation+quality filter AND showing broad value (multiple independent imports or curator spot-check).
- **Creation is hybrid-async**: text/cache-hit finish inline; long ASR jobs run in the background with a completion notification. A failed creation never consumes a Creation Credit.

### Learning loop, recommendation & gamification

- **Bilingual Passage** is target-primary, tap-to-reveal; the tap is also the absorption gesture (untapped-at-completion ⇒ Known). Completion = all projected Candidate Items processed.
- **Practice Mode** is layered onto a common Lesson core, switched on by Source type (Listening Replay for audio; Bilingual reading for text). Listening defaults to audio-first/blind with an optional karaoke toggle.
- **Recommendation** sources next Lesson in priority A (current Series) → B (pool) → D (learner's own Sources); C (on-the-fly web) deferred. Matching uses per-skill Level + **Interest Profile** (topic/author/keyword/format), mostly i+1 but may ease for momentum. Every recommendation shows a **Recommendation Reason** + match %.
- **Interest Profile** seeded at onboarding (topics) and steered by the **Preference Tuner** (shown periodically / on low confidence, below the fold).
- **Progression is continuous (i+1)**, never gated by CEFR bands; crossing a band fires a **Level up** Celebration from behavior, no test. Series are tagged by band for browse/cold-start only.
- **North Star** = cumulative Absorbed Items (Home, largest); distinct from the per-session recap on completion. **Daily Goal** defines the **Streak**; Challenges don't count. **Celebration moments** are two-tier (Daily Goal folded into completion; major milestones full-screen + shareable **Milestone Card**), each with a guilt-free rest exit.
- **Light SRS**: review-in-context (deliberate re-surfacing of Absorbed Items in new Lessons) + optional 60-second quick review; never a due-queue, no red debt badge.

### Challenge Feed (built after the core loop)

- A **Challenge** is a separate entity from a Lesson (no Items, no SRS), authored as structured data (text + Level + topic + background + music) and rendered dynamically in a vertical swipe feed; the same data feeds an **MP4 export pipeline** (watermark + app link). CTA "go deeper" appears at the end of each Challenge; right-rail like/save/share. Its own Home tab.

### Plans & billing

- **Free Plan**: one full Starter Series + monthly Creation Credits (text generous, audio tight). **Paid Plan**: $16/mo or ~$119/yr (annual highlighted), removes consumption cap, raises creation, unlocks podcast listening and offline download. No countdown trial — Free is the trial; **soft, contextual paywall** with a trigger-matched headline. At MVP, sold via app stores (absorb Apple's 15% Small Business fee); web billing later.
- **Offline** is download-and-sync (not offline-first), Paid-only; the Golden First Lesson ships bundled in-app.

## Testing Decisions

A good test asserts **external behavior at the highest seam**, with infrastructure (LLM, ASR, translation, content fetch, app stores, push) faked at injected ports — never asserting internal implementation detail. New seams are introduced at use-case boundaries because this is greenfield.

- **Lesson Creation Engine** — primary seam `createLesson(SourceInput) → Lesson[]` with all adapters (`ContentFetcher`, `Analyzer`, `GrammarMatcher`, `ChunkMatcher`, `Translator`, `AsrAligner`, `SourceCache`) faked. Assert: Candidate Items have the correct type and canonical form; Grammar Points resolve to Inventory entries; Chunks are canonicalized and tiered (anchor vs candidate); long Sources segment into ~3–5-min Lessons while short ones don't; a matching fingerprint yields a cache hit and skips analysis; failed creation consumes no Credit; private text/file Sources are never marked pool-eligible; illegal content is blocked at the personal layer.
- **Recommendation** — seam `nextLesson(learner, justCompleted?) → recommendation` with a fixed catalog. Assert the A→B→D priority, per-skill Level matching, Interest Profile influence (including occasional ease-for-momentum), and that a Reason + match % accompany the result.
- **Absorption & completion** — seam over the Lesson session reducer/state. Assert that tapping an Item marks it Absorbed and New→Learning, untapped Items become Known at completion, completion requires all projected Items processed, and the North Star increments only by genuinely Absorbed Items.
- **Levels & progression** — assert too-easy/too-hard flags move the fine score, a band crossing fires a single Level up event, and Reading/Listening levels move independently.
- **Plans & Credits** — assert Free limits (Starter Series open, Credit decrement by source-type weight, no decrement on failure), contextual soft-paywall triggers, and that offline download is gated to Paid.
- **Challenge Feed** — assert Challenges never produce Items, never count toward the Streak, and the CTA targets a Level/topic-matched Lesson.

Prior art: none yet (greenfield). The first engine test established becomes the reference pattern (port-faked, behavior-asserting) for the rest.

## Out of Scope

- On-the-fly web generation of Lessons (recommendation source C).
- Web app and web billing (and recovering the store fee).
- TTS-ing text Sources for listening practice.
- Sharing "a Lesson I made" (bridge type C) and re-hosting full copyrighted content.
- Automated placement testing.
- Multi–language pairs beyond VI→EN (modeled but not enabled).
- Fuzzy/semantic near-duplicate Source matching.
- A standalone, fully personalized infinite Lesson Feed (Discover ships as an expandable list first).
- The Challenge Feed itself ships after the core learning loop.

## Further Notes

- **Build order**: the Lesson Creation Engine first (it is both the user feature and the curated-content production line), then the Grammar/Chunk Inventories as a content asset, then Golden First Lessons + one Starter Series, then the listening ASR pipeline (highest technical risk — start early to surface problems).
- **Activation metric** = ≥3 Lessons completed within the first 3 days; **retention North Star** = D7. Optimize activation/retention before optimizing the paywall.
- **App name: Inflow** — encodes comprehensible **in**put + staying **in flow** ("Ella" in mockups was a placeholder).
- The global Source cache is the cost backbone that makes both a generous Free tier and in-MVP podcast ASR affordable.
